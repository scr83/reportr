## Step 2 – Set Up Your Agency Branding

Proper branding configuration ensures your white label SEO reports reinforce your agency's professional image with every client delivery. Professional white label SEO reports depend on consistent, high-quality branding throughout every page.

**Logo Upload Process:**

1. Access your tool's branding or settings section

2. Upload your agency logo using the recommended dimensions

3. Preview how the logo appears on report headers and covers

4. Adjust logo positioning if customization options exist

**Logo Best Practices:**

- **File Format**: PNG with transparent background preferred, JPG acceptable
- **Dimensions**: 400x100 pixels provides optimal quality without oversized files
- **File Size**: Keep under 1MB for fast report generation
- **Design**: Ensure logo remains legible at smaller sizes used in report headers

**Brand Color Configuration:**

1. Locate the color customization section in your tool settings

2. Enter your primary brand color using the hex code format (#3B82F6)

3. Add secondary color if the platform supports multiple brand colors

4. Preview color application across charts, graphs, and report elements

5. Save branding settings and confirm changes apply globally

**Company Information Setup:**

- Enter your agency name exactly as it should appear on client reports
- Add primary contact information for report footers
- Include website URL if supported by the platform
- Set default report title formatting preferences

**Professional Appearance Tips:**

- Use your primary brand color consistently across all visual elements
- Ensure logo maintains readability when displayed at various sizes
- Keep contact information current and professional
- Test branding on sample reports before client delivery

These branding elements should align with your overall [agency service presentation](/services) for consistency across all client touchpoints.

**Time Investment**: 2-3 minutes for complete branding setup.

## Step 3 – Connect Google Search Console

Google Search Console provides the foundation data for professional white label SEO reports, including keyword rankings, click-through rates, and organic performance metrics.

**Connection Process:**

1. **Access Integration Settings**: Navigate to your tool's Google integrations or data sources section

2. **Initiate GSC Connection**: Click "Connect Google Search Console" or similar button

3. **Google Authentication**: Sign in using the Google account with Search Console property access

4. **Grant Permissions**: Authorize API access when prompted by Google's OAuth screen

5. **Select Property**: Choose the correct Search Console property from the dropdown menu

6. **Verify Connection**: Confirm the connection shows "Connected" status with a green indicator

**Success Indicators:**

- Green "Connected" badge appears next to the property name
- Property URL matches your client's website exactly
- Recent data appears in preview sections (clicks, impressions, keywords)
- No error messages or "Reconnect" prompts display

**What Search Console Data Provides:**

Google Search Console integration supplies essential metrics for white label SEO reports including total clicks and impressions over the reporting period, average click-through rates and position data, top-performing keywords with individual metrics and position changes, and top-performing pages driving organic traffic.

**Common Connection Issues:**

- **Permission Errors**: Ensure your Google account has Owner or Full User access to the Search Console property
- **Property Selection**: Verify you're selecting the correct property (www vs non-www, HTTP vs HTTPS)
- **Data Delays**: New properties may require 24-48 hours before data appears

**Time Investment**: 1-2 minutes for successful connection.

Properties with existing Search Console data display metrics immediately after connection. New properties may show limited data until Google accumulates sufficient search performance information. This data forms the foundation of your [comprehensive SEO reporting strategy](/blog/comprehensive-seo-reporting-strategy).

## Step 4 – Connect Google Analytics 4

Google Analytics 4 integration adds crucial user behavior metrics that complement Search Console data, providing a complete picture of organic traffic performance in your white label SEO reports.

**GA4 Connection Steps:**

1. **Access Analytics Integration**: Locate Google Analytics 4 connection option in your tool's settings

2. **Authenticate Account**: Sign in with the Google account having GA4 property access

3. **Authorize Permissions**: Grant read access to Analytics data when prompted

4. **Select GA4 Property**: Choose the correct Analytics property from available options

5. **Configure Data Views**: Set up organic traffic filtering if available in your tool

6. **Verify Data Flow**: Confirm user and session data appears correctly

**Critical GA4 Metrics for SEO Reports:**

Google Analytics 4 provides user behavior insights that enhance white label SEO reports including total users and sessions from organic search channels, bounce rate and average session duration metrics, goal completions and conversion tracking data, and top landing pages from organic traffic sources.

**Data Filtering Importance:**

Professional white label SEO reports should focus specifically on organic traffic performance. Ensure your tool filters GA4 data to exclude direct, paid, and social traffic when generating SEO-focused reports.

**Troubleshooting GA4 Connections:**

- **Property Access**: Verify your Google account has Editor or Viewer permissions for the GA4 property
- **Property Type**: Connect to GA4 properties, not Universal Analytics (deprecated)
- **Data Processing**: New GA4 properties require 24-48 hours for initial data processing

**Time Investment**: 1-2 minutes for connection and configuration.

## Step 5 – Configure PageSpeed Insights (Optional but Recommended)

PageSpeed Insights integration adds Core Web Vitals data to your white label SEO reports, addressing the growing importance of site performance in search rankings.

**Why Include PageSpeed Data:**

Core Web Vitals became a ranking factor in 2021, making site speed a critical component of comprehensive SEO reporting. Including performance metrics in white label SEO reports demonstrates thorough analysis and provides actionable insights for clients.

**PageSpeed Connection Process:**

1. **Enable PageSpeed Integration**: Locate PageSpeed Insights option in your tool settings

2. **API Configuration**: Most tools handle API access automatically without requiring manual setup

3. **URL Configuration**: Verify the tool will test your client's primary pages (homepage, key landing pages)

4. **Mobile vs Desktop**: Confirm both mobile and desktop scores will be included

**Core Web Vitals Metrics:**

PageSpeed Insights provides three critical Core Web Vitals measurements including Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) measuring loading performance, First Input Delay (FID) tracking interactivity responsiveness, and Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) indicating visual stability.

**Performance Score Interpretation:**

- **Good Performance**: Scores 90+ (green)
- **Needs Improvement**: Scores 50-89 (orange)  
- **Poor Performance**: Scores 0-49 (red)

**Time Investment**: 30 seconds for configuration.

PageSpeed data adds significant value to white label SEO reports by identifying technical SEO opportunities that directly impact search rankings and user experience. This performance data integrates seamlessly with your [technical SEO audit processes](/blog/technical-seo-audit-checklist).

## Tips for Creating Reports Clients Actually Read

Professional white label SEO reports require more than just data compilation—they need strategic presentation that clients understand and value. The most effective white label SEO reports combine comprehensive data with clear, actionable insights.

**Executive Summary Strategy:**

Place the most important insights on the first page. Clients often read only the summary, so include key wins, notable improvements, and priority recommendations in this section. Use bullet points and clear language rather than technical jargon.

**Highlight Wins and Progress:**

Lead with positive developments including ranking improvements for target keywords, organic traffic growth percentages, and successful optimization results. Position challenges as opportunities with specific action plans.

**Use Period-Over-Period Comparisons:**

Clients need context to understand performance. Include month-over-month comparisons showing traffic trends, quarter-over-quarter ranking improvements, and year-over-year growth when available. Visual charts make trends more apparent than raw numbers.

**Include Actionable Next Steps:**

Every white label SEO report should conclude with specific recommendations including priority optimization opportunities, technical issues requiring attention, and content suggestions based on keyword performance data.

**Limit Technical Jargon:**

Write for business owners, not SEO professionals. Explain metrics in business terms and focus on outcomes rather than processes. Use phrases like "search visibility" instead of "average position" and "website visitors" instead of "organic sessions."

**Consistent Delivery Schedule:**

Send white label SEO reports on the same day each month. Consistency builds expectation and demonstrates systematic approach to optimization. Monthly white label SEO reports work best for most clients, aligning with standard [client communication schedules](/blog/client-communication-best-practices).

**Professional Presentation Tips:**

- Use your agency branding consistently throughout
- Include brief explanations of metrics that may be unfamiliar
- Provide contact information for questions or discussions
- Keep reports focused on performance rather than tool capabilities
