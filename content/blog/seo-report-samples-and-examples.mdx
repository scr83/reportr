## What a Professional SEO Report Sample Includes

Professional SEO report samples follow a consistent structure that balances comprehensiveness with readability. The most effective reports include seven core sections, each serving a specific purpose in the client communication process.

**Essential Report Structure:**

**1. Executive Summary (One-page overview)**
- Key performance indicators for the reporting period
- Period-over-period comparison metrics
- Top 3 wins and top 3 areas for improvement
- Overall progress toward stated SEO goals

**2. Organic Traffic Analysis (2-3 pages)**
- Total organic sessions with trend visualization
- Traffic source breakdown (branded vs. non-branded search)
- Device performance (desktop vs. mobile)
- Geographic performance data

**3. Keyword Rankings & Performance (2-3 pages)**
- Top 10 performing keywords with position tracking
- New keyword rankings achieved during the period
- Lost rankings that require attention
- Keyword opportunity analysis

**4. Top Pages Analysis (1-2 pages)**
- Highest-traffic organic landing pages
- Page performance trends (improving vs. declining)
- Content optimization opportunities
- User engagement metrics per page

**5. Technical Performance (1-2 pages)**
- PageSpeed Insights scores for key pages
- Core Web Vitals data and trends
- Mobile usability metrics
- Critical technical issues identified

**6. Recommendations & Next Steps (1-2 pages)**
- Prioritized action items for the upcoming period
- Expected impact and timeline for each recommendation
- Resource requirements and next steps
- Progress on previous month's recommendations

**7. Appendix & Data References (1-2 pages)**
- Methodology notes and data source attribution
- Contact information and next meeting schedule
- Detailed metrics tables for reference

This structure ensures every report tells a complete story: where the website stands now, how it's performing compared to previous periods, what's working well, what needs attention, and what actions will drive improvement.

The key is maintaining consistent section organization while customizing content and recommendations for each client's specific situation and goals.

## Sample SEO Report: Executive Summary Section

The executive summary makes or breaks client engagement with your reports. This single page determines whether clients read the full report or just skim for numbers. Effective executive summaries present key insights at a glance while creating clear connections between SEO performance and business outcomes.

**Sample Executive Summary Format:**

**Report Period: February 2026**  
**Client: [Company Name]**  
**Prepared by: [Your Agency]**

**Key Performance Overview:**
- Organic Sessions: 4,247 (+12% vs. January)
- Organic Leads: 89 (+18% vs. January)
- Top 10 Rankings: 47 keywords (+5 vs. January)
- Page Speed Score: 78 (improved from 72)

**This Month's Wins:**
1. **New First-Page Rankings:** Achieved page 1 positions for "commercial litigation attorney" and "business dispute lawyer" driving 340 additional monthly sessions
2. **Mobile Performance:** Improved Core Web Vitals across 15 key landing pages, increasing mobile conversion rate by 23%
3. **Content Strategy:** New practice area pages generated 67 qualified leads, exceeding monthly target by 34%

**Priority Areas for March:**
1. **Technical Optimization:** Address remaining Core Web Vitals issues on 8 underperforming pages
2. **Content Expansion:** Develop FAQ content targeting 12 high-intent keywords identified in analysis
3. **Local SEO:** Optimize Google Business Profile to improve local search visibility

**Business Impact Summary:**
February's SEO improvements generated an estimated $23,400 in additional monthly revenue based on average client value and conversion rates. The 18% increase in organic leads represents 16 additional qualified prospects compared to January performance.

**Effective Executive Summary Elements:**

**Visual Hierarchy:**
Clear section headers, consistent formatting, and strategic use of bold text help executives scan quickly for relevant information.

**Period Comparison:**
Every metric includes comparison data (month-over-month, year-over-year) with clear directional indicators (+ for positive, - for negative changes).

**Business Language:**
Technical metrics are connected to business outcomes. Instead of "CTR improved 2.3%," effective summaries state "organic click-through rate improvements generated 89 additional website visitors."

**Forward-Looking Focus:**
Executive summaries don't just report what happened—they preview what's planned and what results to expect.

This format works because busy executives can understand SEO performance and business impact in under two minutes while still providing enough detail to support strategic decisions.

## Sample SEO Report: Keyword & Rankings Section

The keyword rankings section transforms raw position data into actionable insights about search visibility and opportunity. Effective samples demonstrate how to present ranking information that helps clients understand both current performance and future potential.

**Sample Keyword Performance Layout:**

**Top Performing Keywords (Current Month)**

| Keyword | Current Position | Previous Position | Change | Monthly Searches | Est. Monthly Traffic |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|---------|------------------|---------------------|
| business attorney | 3 | 5 | +2 | 1,200 | 247 |
| commercial litigation | 7 | 12 | +5 | 800 | 89 |
| contract disputes | 4 | 4 | — | 650 | 134 |
| employment law firm | 9 | 15 | +6 | 900 | 67 |
| corporate legal counsel | 2 | 3 | +1 | 400 | 89 |

**New Rankings Achieved:**
- "business dispute resolution" → Position 8 (new ranking)
- "commercial contract attorney" → Position 12 (new ranking)
- "corporate litigation services" → Position 6 (new ranking)

**Rankings to Monitor:**
- "intellectual property lawyer" dropped from position 4 to position 9
- "merger and acquisition attorney" declined from position 7 to position 11

**Sample Analysis Text:**

"This month's keyword performance shows strong momentum in commercial litigation terms, with average position improvements of 4.2 positions across targeted keywords. The most significant win was achieving first-page visibility for 'business attorney,' our highest-volume target keyword, which now drives an estimated 247 monthly visitors.

New rankings in dispute resolution terms align with our content strategy focusing on commercial conflict topics. These three new rankings add approximately 78 monthly organic visitors to the website.

The decline in intellectual property rankings requires immediate attention, as this represents our second-highest converting keyword category. We recommend updating the IP practice page with fresh case studies and expanding FAQ content to recapture lost visibility."

**Key Elements in Professional Samples:**

**Tabular Data Presentation:**
Clean tables with consistent formatting make it easy to scan performance across multiple keywords. Include position changes, search volume, and estimated traffic impact.

**Trend Visualization:**
Many samples include simple arrow indicators (↗ ↘ →) or color coding (green for improvements, red for declines) to make changes immediately visible.

**Business Context:**
Raw position numbers don't mean much to clients. Effective samples translate rankings into traffic estimates and business value.

**Strategic Narrative:**
The best keyword sections tell a story about what's working, what's not, and why. This narrative helps clients understand how ranking changes connect to broader SEO strategy.

## Sample SEO Report: Traffic & Pages Section

The traffic and pages section demonstrates how SEO improvements translate into website visitor behavior and business outcomes. Professional samples focus on actionable insights rather than overwhelming clients with every available metric.

**Sample Traffic Analysis Format:**

**Organic Traffic Overview:**
- Total Organic Sessions: 4,247 (February 2026)
- Previous Period: 3,789 sessions (January 2026)
- Growth Rate: +12.1% month-over-month
- 12-Month Average: 3,456 sessions
- Performance vs. Average: +22.9% above trend

**Traffic Quality Metrics:**
- Average Session Duration: 2:34 (+18 seconds vs. January)
- Pages per Session: 2.8 (+0.3 vs. January)
- Bounce Rate: 42.1% (-3.2% vs. January)
- Goal Conversion Rate: 2.1% (+0.4% vs. January)

**Top Organic Landing Pages:**

| Page | Sessions | Sessions Change | Avg. Time | Bounce Rate | Conversions |
|------|----------|-----------------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| /practice-areas/business-law | 789 | +23% | 3:12 | 38% | 18 |
| /services/contract-review | 567 | +8% | 2:45 | 45% | 12 |
| /blog/business-dispute-guide | 445 | +156% | 4:23 | 29% | 9 |
| /attorneys/commercial-litigation | 398 | +5% | 2:18 | 52% | 8 |
| /contact | 234 | +12% | 1:45 | 67% | 31 |

**Sample Analysis Narrative:**

"February's organic traffic growth of 12.1% represents the fourth consecutive month of improvement, with total sessions now 22.9% above our 12-month average. This sustained growth indicates our keyword targeting and content strategy are successfully capturing increased search demand.

The standout performer is our business dispute guide blog post, which generated 156% more traffic than January. This page is attracting high-quality visitors (4:23 average session duration, 29% bounce rate) and converting at a strong rate with 9 goal completions.

User engagement metrics improved across all categories, suggesting that increased traffic quality matches quantity improvements. The 18-second increase in average session duration and 3.2% reduction in bounce rate indicate visitors are finding more relevant, engaging content.

**Page-Level Recommendations:**
1. **Expand high-performing content:** Create additional guides similar to the business dispute post that's driving quality traffic and conversions
2. **Optimize underperforming pages:** The commercial litigation attorney page has a high bounce rate (52%) and low conversion rate—review content relevance and call-to-action placement
3. **Internal linking opportunities:** Connect related practice area pages to capture more traffic from successful landing pages"

**Professional Sample Elements:**

**Context Over Raw Numbers:**
Instead of listing every available metric, professional samples focus on the 5-7 metrics that actually matter for business decisions.

**Trend Analysis:**
Effective samples always include comparison periods and explain what trends mean for the business trajectory.

**Performance Diagnosis:**
The best samples identify both successes to replicate and problems that need attention, with specific recommendations for each.

**Business Translation:**
Traffic numbers are connected to business outcomes—leads, conversions, revenue impact—rather than presented as abstract metrics.

This approach helps clients understand how SEO drives actual business results rather than just increasing vanity metrics.

## Sample SEO Report: Technical Performance Section

Technical performance sections in professional samples focus on metrics that directly impact user experience and search rankings. Rather than overwhelming clients with every technical detail, effective samples highlight issues that affect business performance and provide clear action items.

**Sample Technical Performance Layout:**

**PageSpeed Performance Summary:**
- Desktop Speed Score: 78 (Good - improved from 72)
- Mobile Speed Score: 65 (Needs Improvement - improved from 58)
- Pages Analyzed: 25 key landing pages
- Critical Issues Resolved: 12 (image optimization, unused CSS removal)

**Core Web Vitals Status:**

| Metric | Desktop Performance | Mobile Performance | Status | Target |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|---------|---------|
| Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) | 1.8s | 2.9s | Good/Needs Work | <2.5s |
| First Input Delay (FID) | 89ms | 127ms | Good/Good | <100ms |
| Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) | 0.08 | 0.15 | Good/Needs Work | <0.1 |

**Technical Issues Identified:**
- **High Priority:** 3 pages with mobile CLS scores above 0.1 (layout shift issues)
- **Medium Priority:** 8 pages with LCP scores between 2.5-3.0 seconds
- **Low Priority:** 12 pages with minor JavaScript optimization opportunities

**Sample Technical Analysis:**

"February's technical improvements show significant progress in page speed performance, with desktop scores improving from 72 to 78 and mobile scores advancing from 58 to 65. These improvements directly contributed to the 23% increase in mobile conversion rates reported in the traffic section.

The most critical technical issue requiring immediate attention is Cumulative Layout Shift on mobile devices. Three key landing pages (/practice-areas/business-law, /services/contract-review, /attorneys/team) are experiencing layout shifts above Google's recommended 0.1 threshold. This affects user experience and search rankings.

**Priority Technical Tasks for March:**
1. **Fix mobile layout shifts:** Implement CSS height attributes for images and ads on the three affected pages
2. **Optimize mobile LCP:** Compress hero images and implement lazy loading for below-fold content
3. **JavaScript cleanup:** Remove unused JavaScript files identified on 12 pages to improve loading speed

**Business Impact of Technical Improvements:**
Page speed improvements in February correlated with a 0.4% increase in goal conversion rate and 18-second improvement in average session duration. Addressing remaining mobile issues should continue this positive trend."

**Effective Technical Section Elements:**

**Performance Scoring:**
Using recognizable scoring systems (Google PageSpeed Insights scale) helps clients understand technical performance without requiring technical expertise.

**Priority Classification:**
Grouping issues by priority (high/medium/low) helps clients understand what needs immediate attention versus what can be addressed over time.

**Business Connection:**
The best technical sections connect performance improvements to business metrics (conversion rates, user engagement) rather than presenting technical data in isolation.

**Visual Status Indicators:**
Simple status indicators (Good/Needs Improvement) make it easy for non-technical clients to understand overall technical health.

This format demystifies technical SEO for business stakeholders while still providing actionable information for implementation teams.

## Sample SEO Report: Recommendations & Next Steps

The recommendations section separates professional reports from data dumps. This section transforms SEO analysis into actionable business strategy, showing clients exactly what needs to happen next and what results to expect.

**Sample Recommendations Format:**

**March 2026 SEO Priorities**

**High Priority (Immediate Action Required):**

**1. Fix Mobile Core Web Vitals Issues**
- **Objective:** Resolve Cumulative Layout Shift problems on 3 key landing pages
- **Expected Impact:** 15-20% improvement in mobile conversion rate
- **Timeline:** Complete by March 15
- **Resources Required:** 8 hours development time
- **Success Metrics:** All pages achieve CLS scores below 0.1

**2. Expand High-Converting Content**
- **Objective:** Create 4 additional guide pages similar to business dispute guide
- **Expected Impact:** 200-300 additional monthly organic sessions
- **Timeline:** Publish 1 guide per week throughout March
- **Resources Required:** Content writing and design support
- **Success Metrics:** Each guide achieves 100+ monthly sessions within 60 days

**Medium Priority (Address Within 30 Days):**

**3. Optimize Underperforming Practice Pages**
- **Objective:** Improve conversion rates on commercial litigation and IP law pages
- **Expected Impact:** 25% increase in form submissions from these pages
- **Timeline:** Updates complete by March 31
- **Resources Required:** Content revision and UX improvements
- **Success Metrics:** Reduce bounce rate below 45%, increase time on page above 2:30

**4. Build Local SEO Foundation**
- **Objective:** Optimize Google Business Profile and local citations
- **Expected Impact:** Improved visibility for "attorney near me" searches
- **Timeline:** Foundation complete by March 31
- **Resources Required:** Local SEO audit and optimization
- **Success Metrics:** Appear in local 3-pack for 5 target local keywords

**Low Priority (Consider for April):**

**5. Technical JavaScript Optimization**
- **Objective:** Remove unused JavaScript files from 12 pages
- **Expected Impact:** Minor page speed improvements
- **Timeline:** Address in Q2 development sprint
- **Resources Required:** Technical audit and cleanup
- **Success Metrics:** 5-10 point PageSpeed score improvement

**Sample Strategic Commentary:**

"March's recommendations focus on capitalizing on February's momentum while addressing the technical issues that could limit future growth. The high-priority items directly address our two biggest opportunities: mobile user experience and content expansion.

The mobile Core Web Vitals fixes are critical because 67% of your organic traffic comes from mobile devices, and Google uses mobile experience as a ranking factor. Resolving these issues should maintain our ranking improvements while boosting conversion rates.

Content expansion builds on the success of the business dispute guide, which generated 156% more traffic than expected. Creating similar guides for other practice areas should replicate this success across different keyword categories.

**Expected Combined Impact:**
Completing high and medium priority recommendations should result in:
- 400-500 additional monthly organic sessions
- 20-25% improvement in mobile conversion rate
- 5-8 additional qualified leads per month
- Continued ranking improvements for target keywords"

**Professional Recommendations Elements:**

**Clear Prioritization:**
Effective recommendations are organized by priority and urgency, helping clients allocate resources appropriately.

**Specific Objectives:**
Vague recommendations like "improve SEO" don't help clients. Professional samples include specific, measurable objectives.

**Resource Planning:**
Good recommendations include time and resource estimates so clients can plan implementation effectively.

**Expected Outcomes:**
Each recommendation includes expected business impact, helping clients understand ROI before investing resources.

**Timeline Accountability:**
Clear timelines create accountability for both the agency and client, ensuring recommendations don't get ignored.

This systematic approach to recommendations helps clients view SEO as a strategic business function rather than a mysterious technical process.
